Stories Behind the Numbers

🏔️

Bhutan - Secure Access from Remote Colleges

With eduroam and national identity federation deployed, students across remote campuses gained secure access to global research and learning platforms. These foundations enabled Bhutan's active role in multi-country virtual classrooms with Bangladesh and Nepal, proving that students in the mountains can participate in the same online seminars as those in capital cities.

👩‍💻

Bangladesh - Women in Cybersecurity

The TechGirl programme trained young women in cybersecurity and advanced networking. Graduates have since co-delivered peer trainings in neighbouring countries, growing a regional community of women digital leaders and expanding the talent pipeline for NRENs and universities.

🏥

Pakistan - Telemedicine for Remote Clinics

A WP5-funded platform linked rural clinics with specialists in urban centres via a central hub. Patients in underserved areas received faster diagnoses and follow-up care, and the model helped spark wider policy conversations about digital health equity.

☁️

Vietnam - Research Data Management

A cloud-based research data platform across VinaREN institutions gave researchers structured access to shared repositories and collaboration tools such as eduroam and federated identity. It moved institutions toward open-science readiness and better stewardship of research outputs.

🌍

LandSAGE - Environmental Resilience

Initially developed for disaster risk in Southeast Asia, LandSAGE added modules for urban flooding, haze tracking, and pandemic mapping. Through cross-border exchanges, teams localised the platform for Nepal and Mongolia, demonstrating how a regional tool can be adapted for diverse ecosystems.

📡

Nepal - Network Transformation

Nepal's link capacity grew from just 45 Mbps to 1,000 Mbps (1 Gbps) - a 22× increase that enabled researchers and students to access global resources at speeds never before possible. This transformation brought Kathmandu into the global research ecosystem.

Detailed Work Package Structure

WP1 - Network Procurement & Management

+

Focused on establishing and maintaining core backbone connectivity. Absorbed ~45% of the project budget, reflecting the priority of continuously enhancing the e-infrastructure. TEIN*CC managed WP1 centrally, negotiating capacity contracts that dramatically lowered unit bandwidth costs for partners.

  • Upgraded backbone links to 10–100 Gbps
  • Added 100G Asia-Europe links (Singapore – London, Singapore – Hong Kong)
  • Established Asia-Pacific Europe "AER" Ring for redundancy
  • Network capacity grew from 107 Gbps to 926 Gbps (8.7× growth)

WP2 - Capacity Development of Developing NRENs

+

Strengthened human and organizational capacity of NRENs in Least Developed and Lower-Middle Income countries through training programs, workshops, and staff exchanges.

  • Train-the-trainer approaches to multiply impact locally
  • Foundation courses for engineers from Bhutan, Nepal, Laos
  • Annual NREN NOC (Network Operations Centre) trainings
  • NREN business models and stakeholder engagement training

WP3 - Network Design & Advanced Services

+

Designed new R&E network architectures and introduced advanced services alongside higher-level technical training.

  • Deployed eduroam roaming Wi-Fi and identity federations
  • Established distributed AI/Big Data testbed across multiple countries
  • Provided hands-on training in network security and cloud services
  • Science DMZ for big data optimization

WP4 - Applications, Services & Community Building

+

Deployed specialized network applications and fostered user communities around collaboration tools and platforms.

  • Rollout of eduroam to Myanmar, Vietnam and other countries
  • Identity federation services for single sign-on
  • Teleconferencing systems and digital libraries
  • Regional working groups in security and cloud computing

WP5 - Innovation for Societal Benefit

+

Promoted research and education collaborations that directly benefit society, especially in health, agriculture, environment, and culture.

  • Telemedicine networks for remote diagnostics
  • Environmental monitoring systems and air quality sensors
  • Smart city air monitoring for public health
  • Disaster management using space data applications

WP6 - Bridging the Digital Divide

+

Extended internet access to underserved rural and remote areas in developing countries, reducing connectivity inequities.

  • Community-managed networks in Pakistan's Gilgit-Baltistan
  • Connected villages in Bhutan to campuses in Laos
  • Internet Exchange Points (IXPs) in Nepal and Myanmar
  • Local caching solutions for improved sustainability

Resilience Through Disruption

The programme spanned years of rapid change and significant shocks. Partners dealt with pandemics, natural disasters, and periods of political uncertainty. Even so, services grew and collaborations deepened. Several factors explain this resilience:

🔄 Regional Redundancy

Shared operations reduced service disruption and allowed traffic to be rerouted when needed.

🌐 Communities of Practice

Established networks kept training and collaboration alive online when travel stopped.

🔧 Open Standards

Common tools meant solutions built in one country could be reused in another with minimal friction.

🏠 Local Ownership

When projects proved helpful, teams found ways to keep them going, even in lean times.

Infrastructure

Procurement and operation of the TEIN backbone, resilient routing, international transit, and introduction of common services like IPv6, eduroam, and federated identity.

Project Timeline

2016

Project Launch

Asi@Connect officially started as the fifth phase of TEIN initiative, building on decades of Asia-Europe R&E networking cooperation.

2017

First Call for Proposals

Launched competitive sub-granting mechanism with 12 approved projects focusing on eduroam deployment and telehealth pilots.

2019

Network Expansion

Established Asia-Pacific Europe Ring (AER) with 100 Gbps capacity, dramatically improving network resilience and speed.

2021

Pandemic Response

Network proved crucial for remote learning and telemedicine during COVID-19, with 3x increase in traffic volume.

2025

Project Completion

Successfully delivered on all objectives with lasting impact on regional R&E networking and sustainable development.
